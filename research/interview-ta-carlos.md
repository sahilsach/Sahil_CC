# Interview Transcript: Carlos (TA)
**Date:** November 1, 2025
**Interviewer:** David (PM)
**Duration:** 42 minutes
**Course:** Introduction to Psychology (PSY 101)
**Year:** 4th year PhD student

---

*[Recording starts, sounds like busy TA room, background chatter]*

**David:** Is this a good place to talk?

**Carlos:** It's fine, everyone's got headphones in. Plus I'm kind of the de facto leader here so they're used to me having meetings.

**David:** De facto leader?

**Carlos:** Yeah, I'm the senior TA for Professor Martinez's course. Been doing it longest. Not an official title or anything, but when the other TAs have questions, they come to me.

**David:** Tell me about that.

**Carlos:** Okay so, there are six TAs for intro psych. 280 students. It's a lot to coordinate. Professor Martinez is great but she's insanely busy - research, committees, her own teaching prep. So a lot of the day-to-day TA coordination falls on me.

**David:** What does that look like?

**Carlos:** Everything. Someone's confused about the rubric? They ask me. Someone's running behind? They tell me. Scheduling conflict with office hours? We work it out in our group text. Basically I'm a unpaid manager on top of my own TAing work.

**David:** That sounds like a lot.

**Carlos:** It is. I probably spend... five extra hours a week? Just on coordination stuff. Answering questions, resolving disputes, making sure everyone knows what's happening. That's on top of my own grading, my own research, my dissertation...

*[long pause]*

**Carlos:** And here's the thing - there's no tools for this. Like, GradeFlow is great for individual grading. But it completely ignores the fact that TAing is a team sport. There's no dashboard where I can see how everyone's doing. No communication channel. No way to redistribute work if someone's struggling.

**David:** What do you use instead?

**Carlos:** Group texts. Slack. Google Sheets I made myself. It's all held together with duct tape and good intentions.

**David:** What would you want to see in a tool?

**Carlos:** A "head TA" view. Something that shows me - at a glance - how all six TAs are doing. Who's ahead, who's behind, whose grades are looking weird. Right now I have to manually check in with everyone. "Hey Sarah, how's grading going?" Like I'm their mom.

**David:** *[laughs]* That does sound exhausting.

**Carlos:** It is! And I'm not even getting paid for it. My TA allocation is the same as everyone else's. These coordination hours are just... extra.

*[sound of chair rolling]*

**Carlos:** Can I tell you about the grading inconsistency thing?

**David:** Please.

**Carlos:** Okay so, because I'm senior, I sometimes review the other TAs' grades. Not officially, just spot-checking when something seems off. And I've found some wild inconsistencies.

**David:** Like what?

**Carlos:** Like, two TAs grading the same essay wildly differently. Or one TA who's clearly rushing - grades all their essays in like two hours with one-sentence comments. Or another TA who's being way too harsh compared to everyone else.

**David:** How do you handle that?

**Carlos:** I talk to them. Diplomatically. "Hey, I noticed your grades are a bit lower than average, want to compare notes?" But it's awkward because I'm not actually their supervisor. I'm a peer. They can just ignore me.

**David:** Do they?

**Carlos:** Sometimes. Most people are receptive because they want to do a good job. But I've had TAs get defensive. "Who made you the grading police?" That kind of thing.

**David:** What would make it easier?

**Carlos:** Official data. If GradeFlow showed - objectively - "here's how each TA's grades compare to the average," that's not me being the police. That's just information. People could self-correct without me having to be the bad guy.

**David:** That makes sense.

**Carlos:** And alerts! Like, if a TA's grades are drifting way off, tell Professor Martinez automatically. Don't make me be the one to notice and escalate. That's uncomfortable.

*[pause]*

**Carlos:** Actually, speaking of uncomfortable - there's the workload thing too.

**David:** Tell me about that.

**Carlos:** So last semester, one of the TAs - I won't name names - basically stopped showing up. Like, just stopped doing work. And I was the one who noticed because suddenly students were asking me about grades that should have been done weeks ago.

**David:** What happened?

**Carlos:** Turns out they had some personal stuff going on. Which is totally understandable! But they didn't tell anyone. And because there's no tracking, nobody knew until it became a crisis. I ended up doing most of their grading on top of my own.

**David:** That's a lot.

**Carlos:** It was brutal. But what else was I supposed to do? Let students wait forever for grades? Tell Professor Martinez and throw my colleague under the bus? There was no good option.

**David:** What would have helped?

**Carlos:** Early warning. Like, if the system noticed "hey, this person hasn't entered any grades in two weeks" and flagged it automatically. Not as a punishment but as a check-in. "Is everything okay? Do you need help?" Takes the burden off me to be the detective.

**David:** That's a theme I'm hearing from a lot of people.

**Carlos:** I bet. Because right now the system just assumes everyone is fine and doing their work. And most of the time that's true! But when it's not true, it becomes my problem to solve.

*[background noise increases]*

**Carlos:** Hold on... *[talking to someone else]* Yeah, just put it on the shared drive, I'll look at it later.

**David:** Sounds like you're in demand.

**Carlos:** *[laughs]* Always. This is what I mean about being the unofficial manager. Everyone needs something from me.

**David:** Do you like that role?

**Carlos:** Honestly? Kind of. I like helping people and I like feeling useful. But I'd like it more if it was recognized and supported. Right now I'm doing manager work with intern tools.

**David:** What tools would help?

**Carlos:** Communication hub for TAs, not Slack-dependent. Workload tracker that shows who's ahead, who's behind. Grading consistency dashboard. Ability to redistribute work when someone's struggling. Basically, treat TAing like the team effort it is instead of six individuals working in isolation.

**David:** That's really helpful feedback.

**Carlos:** Happy to share. And look, I'm graduating next year, so this won't help me personally. But the next person in this role - whoever becomes the informal leader - they deserve better tools.

**David:** Thank you Carlos.

**Carlos:** Sure thing. Let me know if you build something cool. I'll be first in line to test it.

*[recording ends]*
